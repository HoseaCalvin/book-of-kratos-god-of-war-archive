Vanaheim

Realm of the Vanir

The Nature's Gift

A tapestry of the cosmology, conjured in the form of lush forests and greener bushes.
The haven of all living creatures, Vanaheim summons those who have a desire to prosper together with nature. Animals whisper their joy, the leaves dance in harmony, it is the impeccable habitat in the Nine Realms. Since eons ago, the Vanir have dedicated themselves to learn, foster, and ally with wildlife, their enigmatic key to prevail in wars against the Aesir.

Major Regions

The Southern Wilds

Situated in the southernmost point, The Southern Wilds serves its purpose as a shelter of animals. Comprising humongous flora and rough landscapes, they are nature's barriers shielding the fauna. Beyond them may they slumber, wander, and feast peacefully.

Freyr's Camp

Throughout the Aesir-Vanir war, the coerced were the Vanir who had to conceal themselves at a strategic location, as a means to survive and prevail.
Hence, a humble abode was built by Freyr and his allies, its utilities crafted from nature's gifts. In the times of silence, they would gather near the glimmering campfire to howl their songs.

Eastern Barri Woods

Adjacent to an abandoned Vanir settlement, Eastern Barri Woods was an important region for its bygone villagers. Now only desecrated temples stand while the Einherjar thrived from its destruction, a grim legacy of the Aesir-Vanir war. Special memories formed within now overlapped by blood and flesh.

Western Barri Woods

Across Eastern Barri Woods is a region called Western Barri Woods, a temporary resting place for the celestial wolves, Sköll and Hati. On the other side lies another prairie, bordered by a wide ravine. The sceneric view, along with the bright sunlight, invites animals and mortals who have longed for ages.

The Northern Wilds

Sitting at the northernmost point, The Northern Wilds is home to a bygone sacred Vanir temple. It was possibly used to host local traditions, or an offering place for the gods. With most of the Vanir parting ways, it no longer holds a significance to the present residents.

The Crater

A land full of fertility eons ago, now it is a barren wilderness known as The Crater.
The souls of the departed attested that a fierce duel between an Aesir an a Jötnar took place, demolishing settlements around. In addition, who faced a dreary fate were the innocent folks at the settlements. As of now, only ghosts and spirits occupy the vacant lands.
