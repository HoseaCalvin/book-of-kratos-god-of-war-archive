Alfheim

Realm of the Elves

The Elven Sanctuary

Major Regions

Lake of Light

The sole conduit into the domain of the light elves, Lake of Light stretches beyond the far-reaching horizons. Featuring resplendent banks, luminous fresh water, and fertile lands, the lake graces the splendor of Alfheim and serves as a lodge to watch over the realm.

The Ringed Temple

The Ringed Temple, also regarded as Temple of Light, is the sacred ground revered by both elven races. Its divine beam shoots at the shimmering sky, imbued with ethereal essence whose origin and power have remained a mystery to all beings.

Temple of Light

Notwithstanding the everlasting clash of the elves, this archaic temple has remained untouched and stood tall. Home to the Lake of Souls, it is the source of Alfheim's light and the resting place of fallen creatures.

The Strönd

Stretching from the elven temple to the canyon of Alfheim, The Strönd is a narrow passage brimmed with uncovered mysteries in Alfheim. Entrenched retreats of dark elves, infested caverns, and elevated terrains make exploration arduous and perilous.

The Barrens

The Barrens, one of the major Alfheim deserts, is a concrete trail of casualties of the elven conflict. It is strewn with monumental remnants, forsaken outposts, and crumbling ruins, a testament to decaying lives and civilization. Even amidst the conflict, the surviving elves are entrenched deep beneath the hollow, windy dunes.

The Forbidden Sands

The Forbidden Sands, another great desert, stands as a decaying legacy due to the atrocities of war. Home to an ancient library and grand structures, it stood as a testament to Alfheim's lost grandeur. The Statue of Freyr, once a beacon of harmony between both sides, now remains only a relic long forsaken.
