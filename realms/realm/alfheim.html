Alfheim

Realm of the Light Elves

The Elven Sanctuary

Major Regions

Lake of Light

The primary conduit to reach the glamorous Temple of Light, Lake of Light connects its grand icon and plethora of hidden pathways.
Ascertain a boat and row through the glistening waterways to gaze at the grandeur elven structures or perhaps unearth exquisite elven treasures.

The Ringed Temple

The main icon of the realm itself, Ringed Temple is the haven of both elven subraces, light elves and dark elves.
Within the center of the sanctuary lies the Light of Alfheim itself, the divine light capable of dispelling dark magic and channeling tremendous power to the Realm Travel Temple. However, the dark elves have overrun the structure and scattered hives that weaken the light.

Temple of Light

Despite the on-going elven war, this temple remains intact and still houses lores of both elves.
The towering ringed structure overlooking the realm, the structure boasting high intricacy contribute to the distinctiveness of the realm. The light is still present, unfortunately, with weaker brightness, signifying its declining energy.

The Strönd

Behind the Temple of Light stretches a narrow pathway that leads to the Alfheim desert called The Strönd.
Nothing ethereal about this eerie place aside of strange creatures, steep terrains, and various chests scattered around. Beware of dark elves or light elves in the vicinity as they tend to be hostile towards unwanted visitors!

The Canyon

The Barrens

One of two major Alfheim deserts, called The Barrens, stands as a remarkable effect of the long-standing conflict. Damaged structures, lain skulls, and dark elven hives are the ultimate cost of war. Hidden in plain sight are stray animals or vile beings, seeking prey on the fragile.

The Forbidden Sands

Sitting next to The Barrens stands another wasteland named The Forbidden Sands. Once was probably a field of blood, the desert is now an elven grave brimmed with hollowness. The breezing wind blows its flute, singing a sorrow song for the fallen elves.
