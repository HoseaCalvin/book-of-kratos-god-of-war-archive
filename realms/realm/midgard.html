Midgard

Realm of the Humans

The Heart of the Nine Realms

Major Regions

Wildwoods

Wildwoods, nestled on the outer reaches of Midgard, are renowned not only for their soothing breeze but also for the ethereal serenity from conflicts within the mortal realm. Encircled by a protection stave imbued with arcane magic, they have withstood ravaging beasts and malevolent intruders for unnumbered winters.

The River Pass

The River Pass, tucked between towering hills, conceals forsaken sanctuaries and secluded resorts. Adorned with vivid, enchanting wonders, it highlights the undiscovered Midgard's panoramic vista.

Lake of Nine

Renowned for its splendor, Lake of Nine is universally regarded as the nexus of the Norse tapestry. Here rests Tyr's Temple, an ethereal bridge between realms, that bears witness to a legacy of fleeting harmony. Encircling the temple, the eight realm towers hold an essential key to each diverse realm.

Foothills

The Foothills, lying at the foot of Midgard's tallest mountain, are feared for their precarious pass and rumbling storms. Once a vital key to reach the summit, the now-abandoned conduit has become a nest of vicious fiends, thus demanding adamantine resolve to surge forth.

The Mountain

The Mountain, whose summit almost pierces through Midgard's atmosphere, unveils the resplendent vistas only visible from its peak. An old legend tells of the last known passage to Jötunheim, shielded by an inexplicable Jötnar magic impervious even to the Allfather's power.

Thamur's Corpse

Thamur, whose exceptional abilities in stonecraft are universally revered, pledged himself in service to aiding all Jötnar refugees in the mortal realm. Unbeknownst to the Jötunn, his monumental work left a subtle trail Thor would one day trace, eventually sealing the fate of Thamur and his confederates.

Veithurgard

A monster-ravaged wasteland with traces of abominable horrors, Veithurgard once stood as a solemn enclosure of a dwarven king, Motsognir, in Midgard. Its meadow, teeming with abundant resources, was an anchor to prosperity and longevity of local farmers who had settled. Unfortunately, the thriving settlement collapsed when the farmers, ill-equipped and innocent, were mutated into treacherous entities.

Konùnsgard

Konùnsgard, whose ruin embodies a stark collapse of Motsognir heritage, was a fortified stronghold in Midgard. The labyrinthine terrains and lingering demons hinder intruders from scavenging its precious relics. A rumor unfolds that its residents, unsuspecting of the king's grand scheme, were sacrificed to forge a legendary armor.

Lower Wildwoods

Lower Wildwoods, whose magical stave has weakened, are vulnerable to intrusions from otherworldly beings to ruthless Norse warlords. At the dawn of Fimbulwinter, entrenched raiders and undead prowlers have plagued the territory, a reflection of decaying order and structure in the realm.

Upper Wildwoods

A desolate forest in the southern reaches of Midgard, Upper Wildwoods once served as an ephemeral habitat for the realm's vivacious fauna. Nevertheless, its essence vanished upon the emergence of Fimbulwinter, blanketing the natural sanctuary in thick snow and unending snowstorms. The wildlife retreated into the depths of the earth, yearning for soothing warmth from the sun.

Lake of Nine

Once a radiant body of water surrounded by lush pastures, the Lake of Nine has succumbed to a frostbitten plain beneath an eternal blizzard, mirroring the dissipation of harmony in the Norse cosmos. Its central monument, Týr's Temple, no longer retains its essence as a bridge between worlds, but a crumbling sanctuary that reflects the emergence of chaos.

Shores of Nine

The Shores of Nine, comprising boundless prairies and soaring hills, represent the decaying splendor of Midgard under the breath of Fimbulwinter. With the untamable frost, the fauna and flora are on the brink of extinction, driving relentless scavengers to devour flesh of the innocent.

Well of Urð

The Well of Urð, the impenetrable threshold of the Nornir, is believed to transcend both space and time. Within their solemn sanctuary, they carefully weave the fate of every life, a testament to their formidable authority that outclasses that of the Allfather. Their existence solidifies an undeniable truth that the future is etched and immutable, untouchable even by the deities.
