Midgard

Realm of the Humans

The Heart of the Nine Realms

Revered by a number of individuals as Earth, Midgard stands at the center of the Nine Realms.
Abundant with biomes, resources, and seasons, it is an unrivaled paradise fit for survival and hosting events. Hence, the Aesir have been present for millennia, seeking to maintain ordinance amongst the mortals. However, Odin, the ruler of Asgard, has provided protection over the powerless by sending Jormungandr, known as Midgard's serpent and protector.

Major Regions

Wildwoods

Within the deep forests and gigantic rocks stands Kratos' new hut, encircled by a magical stave. Abounding with spruce woods and animals, they are a leverage for Kratos and Atreus in channeling their might. Nearby also stands a river, a natural altar for their soul or a tranquil spot to reflect upon their journeys.

The River Pass

A crucible from the nature in the form of mazes, puzzles, deep burrows, and steep climbs. An adventurer's true audacity will unravel the moment their embark through here commences. Those who are lucky may find outstanding rewards, an honor for their unparalleled mettle.

Lake of Nine

A vast lake surrounded by mysterious islands, verdant prairies, and mountain ranges covered in thick clouds. At its center stands a revered temple by all Norse races named Týr's Temple, encircled by eight realm towers. On the other hand, the islands speak of many things, one of which is the dead whose wishes are not yet fulfilled.

Foothills

Those who have a plea to behold Midgard's splendor must embark on a perilous path. Demanding tremendous will, the journey to the zenith is full of life-threatening obstacles. Without one's fiery spirit, a sendoff to Helheim will certainly occur.

The Mountain

On its zenith stands a view worth remembering, a view Midgard's splendor. Another form nature's grace, only bestowed upon the deserving. However, in certain occasions, its comforting wind becomes frozen and transformed into unyielding blizzard.

Thamur's Corpse

A worshipped stonemason of the Jötnar, now dead lying on snow plains. Having had a deep care for his race, Thamur built a bastion around Jötunheim during the Aesir genocide. Unfortunately, his actions later attracted Thor, whom he subsequently battled until his last drop of blood. His falling corpse murdered an entire village, and his last breath freezing the grounds.

Veithurgard

Once a kingdom under the authority of a dwarf, Veithurgard is now forsaken and ravaged by Seiðr monsters. Scrolls and folktowns have unveiled horror secrets of its occupants, all over greed and tyranny. However, the living in Veithurgard were thriving farmers who had reaped lots of food resources. They lived happily to see the sun and the moon on a prosperous land.

Konùnsgard

Having faced similar fate like Veithurgard, Konùnsgard was another region led by the same dwarf. A shelter for a dragon, taking many winters, was constructed for a single goal. Again, terrible rumors about Konùnsgard's residents spread wide, with only selected individuals knowing the full truth. Once was a prosperous land, now a wasteland as a result of greed.

Lower Wildwoods

In the wake of Fimbulwinter, nature falls ill, its warmth now an eternal blizzard. The living now face harsher conditions as law does not rule anymore in Midgard. Undead warriors and monsters have awakened, with all magics now dissipating. In the end, it is a matter of time before doom befalls upon the living.

Upper Wildwoods

Near Lower Wildwoods is an elevated, rugged ground called Upper Wildwoods. Since Fimbulwinter, no singing or humming are heard, signifying the absence of the living. Standing on the snowy surface are now the remnants of the dead or damaged structures.

Lake of Nine

Lake of Nine, once a magnificent lake under the luminous sunlight, now a thin icy floor shrouded in snowstorms. Its grand temple, Týr's Temple, loses its significance and becomes a legacy of the bygone. Additionally, some individuals have gone rogue and settled in its vicinity, as a means to survive.

Shores of Nine

Bordering Lake of Nine, Shores of Nine is home to The Realm Towers, ancient structures, and other minor settlements. The signs of past lives can be attested through artifacts, loot chests, and scrolls. Now spirits reside since the eternal winter, with tears flowing down their face as Midgard falls apart.

Well of Urð

There live the Nornir, who weave the fate of warriors and gods behind the curtains. Shall one seek answers from them, the Nornir will commit ungodly actions to decide the deserving and the undeserving. One must be willing to overcome their gravest sins, their traumas, even their death. For the words of Nornir do not speak of sweet lies but bitter truths that sting the heart.
